How to Write Effective NDT Prompts

Learn how to structure your questions to get the most accurate and helpful responses from the NDT Assistant.

1. Specify Your Region

Always indicate whether you're working under Australian (AS/NZS) or American (ASME) standards.

2. Include Technical Context

  • Material type and thickness
  • Equipment model/type
  • Specific procedure or standard reference
  • Type of inspection being performed
Include relevant parameters like wall thickness, material grade, or operating conditions for more precise guidance.

3. Structure Your Question

"I need guidance on:
- Inspection type: UT shear wave
- Standard: ASME Section V
- Material: Carbon steel pipe
- Thickness: 12mm
- Specific query: DAC curve setup requirements"

4. Sample Prompts

"Under AS/NZS standards, what are the acceptance criteria for: - MT inspection - Carbon steel pressure vessel - T-joint welds - Material thickness: 25mm"
For urgent queries, start your prompt with "URGENT:" to indicate time-critical nature.

5. Follow-up Questions

If you need clarification, reference your previous question and specify what additional information you need.

"Regarding the previous MT acceptance criteria, can you clarify the requirements for: - Linear indications - Maximum allowable cluster length"
